Higher Education Sustainability Management System
(HESMS)

Encourage a culture of continuous improvement by regularly reviewing and updating the HESMS model based on feedback and evolving sustainability practices. The Higher Education Sustainability Management System (HESMS) would serve as a comprehensive framework for higher education institutions in India to plan, execute, and monitor their sustainability initiatives effectively. It promotes collaboration, data-driven decision-making, and alignment with global sustainability goals, ultimately creating environmentally and socially aware citizens and contributing to India's commitment to a sustainable future.
Our Special Calculators
1

CARBON EMISSIONS

Carbon Emissions Calculation is a critical component of sustainable practices and helps organizations and institutions take informed actions to mitigate their impact on climate change and promote environmental sustainability.
Carbon Emissions Calculation is a process used to quantify the amount of carbon dioxide (CO2) and other greenhouse gases (GHGs) released into the atmosphere as a result of various human activities, such as energy consumption, transportation, industrial processes, and more. This calculation is essential for assessing an organization's or institution's environmental impact, understanding its contribution to climate change, and identifying opportunities to reduce emissions.
2

Energy Consumption

This calculation provides a measure of the electricity consumed by the building during the specified time frame and is fundamental for assessing energy efficiency, managing utility costs, and setting energy reduction targets within a Higher Education Sustainability Management System (HESMS).
Energy consumption calculation involves determining the total amount of energy used by an entity (such as a building, institution, or system) over a specified period. The energy consumption is typically measured in units such as kilowatt-hours (kWh) for electricity. Here's a brief overview of energy consumption calculation along with the formula
3

WASTE REDUCTION RATE

Waste Reduction Rate Calculation is a method used to determine the effectiveness of waste management practices in reducing the amount of waste that ends up in landfills or incineration
Overall, the Waste Reduction Rate Calculation is a valuable tool for monitoring and improving waste management practices within a Higher Education Sustainability Management System (HESMS) or any organization committed to sustainability.
4

EETVA-Calculator

This comprehensive formula combines factors related to enrollment, affordability, financial aid, sanitation facilities, and student attendance. It provides a more holistic view of the institution's commitment to ensuring equal access to education and a conducive learning environment, taking into account both financial and non-financial aspects of accessibility and participation.
5

Calculate Your Overall Score

Calculating your carbon footprint involves quantifying greenhouse gas emissions resulting from daily activities. It includes factors like energy use, transportation, and diet. Assessing your footprint raises awareness of your impact on climate change and enables informed choices for a more sustainable lifestyle, contributing to a greener planet.
